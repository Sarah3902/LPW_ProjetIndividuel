THE CONCEPT...

The general concept of the project was to create NFTs on the theme of the abyss in order to present them at the OFFF festival (see the section in question for more information).

Being affected by thalassophobia (fear of the seabed and anything that can hide there), I was not very interested in the imposed subject. I decided to use this fear as a strength and that's why I went with the idea of â€‹â€‹creating NFTs in the shape of more or less scary eyes to represent what can be hidden in the depths of the ocean. Each NFT is made up of eyes, a shadow in the background and a dark background to remind us of the absence of light in the abyss.

OFFF FESTIVAL

THE OFFF FESTIVAL...

The OFFF is an annual design festival in Barcelona (Disseny Hub, Design Museum) that brings together and presents many artists and many conferences for 3 days.

Many artists from all over the world came to present their work and their ways of working, thinking, perceiving design and creativity. The inspiring conferences are in English in order to allow as many people as possible to understand (the public also coming from all over the world).

In addition to the conferences, meeting times are organized, artists/small businesses have stands to sell their products (creative and design) to visitors and the company Adobe also had a stand on which it offered to test the Creative suite. Cloud and any other activity related to design.

This year's edition took place from March 23 to 25, 2023. The work-study students of the Professional Webdesign License, the work-study 2nd year MMIs and some 1st year MMIs traveled to Barcelona to participate in this event.

MY EXPERIENCE

MY EXPERIENCE AT THE OFFF...

It was my first time at the OFFF festival and it was an incredible experience. The place (design museum, Disseny Hub) in Barcelona is original and already plunges into a creative universe upon arrival.

I participated in several conferences over the 3 days, such as that of Gavin Strange, Sarah Boris, Bienal, Google or even some of Adobe on Photoshop and Premiere Pro. Some conferences were more or less interesting and more or less well led by the artists in question, but it was interesting to be able to compare the points of view and the techniques of each.

However, my level of English was far too low to understand everything, especially during the conferences of English-speaking artists (especially Americans) because their English was far too fast and "chew" for me.

Me and Gavin Strange

BENIAL's presentation

My 2 favorite conferences were that of Gavin Strange and that of BENIAL. Gavin Strange is an English artist who has many strings to his bow since he is a creator, producer, mixer, designer, etc... I really liked the way he made his (humorous) presentation and his project of awareness campaign for turtles (related to the ocean, like our NFTs) that he presented to us from sketches of ideas to the final video.

For BENIAL, which is an Indian company if I understood correctly, there were 2 to present, a man and a woman. They decided to turn their presentation in a different way, saying themselves that they didn't just want to present their work to us but rather their life, and that's why they each talked about their passions, their place of life, their family. ... And then "burned" a typical ensens from their home. Their fluency in speaking and their presentation made the whole audience laugh and I think that this also allowed them to retain them, since it was the most "original" conference of the festival.
